Dr. Dipendra Pradhan

Dr. Dipendra Pradhan — also known to many as Kalinkaar is a consultant clinical pathologist currently working at Trishuli Hospital, Nuwakot.

With a calm, methodical approach to laboratory medicine and diagnosis, he supports clinicians and patients by ensuring accurate diagnostic testing, quality laboratory practice, and thoughtful interpretation of results. His clinical work is grounded in patient-first values: reliability, clear communication, and a commitment to improving diagnostic services for his local community.

Beyond the microscope, Dr. Pradhan is an active researcher and writer on the Vedic and Puranic history of Nepal. He explores the intersection of ancient texts, cultural memory, and local tradition, aiming to make complex historical and spiritual material on Nepal, accessible to a modern audience. His studies reflect a careful, inquisitive mindset — applying close reading and contextual thinking rather than simple summary — and contribute to public conversations about Nepal’s cultural heritage.

Dr. Pradhan shares his thoughts and findings through a growing online presence: a dedicated website and a YouTube channel where he posts lectures, short talks, and essays that blend scholarly curiosity with practical insight. On these platforms he speaks to a wide audience — from students and fellow researchers to anyone interested in Nepal’s historical roots — and encourages dialogue and community engagement.

His dual passions — clinical pathology and cultural scholarship — inform one another. The precision, skepticism, and attention to evidence required in laboratory medicine sit comfortably beside the careful textual study he brings to Vedic and Puranic topics. Whether diagnosing a difficult case in the hospital lab or tracing a thread through an ancient text, Dr. Pradhan’s work is characterized by patience, rigor, and a desire to educate.
